8Hook angles for new creative

Winner Video_11 will fatigue in 10-14 days at the budgets above. Have these variants in the can before launch so you can swap without dark days. Each one is a different angle on the same offer.

Angle 1 — Direct outcome (the high performer)

Hook: "Men in Barnsley over 40 are using this 30-minute treatment to fix what Viagra can't."

Body beats: Name the problem (performance / sensitivity / confidence) → name the cause (blood flow, not psychology) → name the mechanic (your own platelets, not drugs) → name the proof (clinic name, GMC, hundreds of men treated) → invite (free consultation in Barnsley).

Why it works: mirrors the Winner Video_11 structure — direct, clinical, no shame.

Angle 2 — The "what your GP won't say"

Hook: "Your GP gave you a 7-minute appointment and a pill. Here's what they're not telling you."

Body beats: Sympathise with NHS frustration → contrast with a 30-minute private treatment → keep it credible (we're a CQC-aligned clinic, not a back-alley operator) → free consult.

Why: The Barnsley demo skews working-class older men. Distrust of brief GP visits is a real emotional anchor.

Angle 3 — Partner-led concern

Hook (female VO over male B-roll): "If you've noticed your husband going quiet about it, this is for him."

Body: Partners are the unspoken decision-driver for P-Shot. Speak directly to wives / partners as the conversation starter. The man calls; the partner often pushed him.

Why: The conversion data shows mostly men, but lifestyle research on this category is clear: female partners trigger the booking phone call.

Angle 4 — Drug-free / no pills

Hook: "No daily pills. No side effects. No prescription. Just a 30-minute treatment that uses your own body."

Body: Direct attack on Viagra/Cialis pain points (timing, side effects, dependency). Plant PRP as the modern alternative.

Why: ED-pill side effects are the #1 organic search complaint in this niche.

Angle 5 — Testimonial / social proof

Hook: Static or short video: "Mark, 52, Barnsley. 'I didn't think it'd work. Then it did.'"

Body: Genuine patient testimonial (anonymised or actor-read) → quick mechanic explainer (15s) → CTA.

Why: Image Ad2 (the static) already won 2 leads at scale. This is the next iteration.

Casting note from the data: The 53 won leads are an ethnically diverse cross-section (South Asian, Eastern European, African, white British). Cast accordingly. Don't shoot only white English men.

10Speed-to-lead — the biggest invisible lever

This matters more than any creative decision. P-Shot is high-shame, high-intent, low-research. The window between "I just submitted" and "I'm calling someone else" is short. Research on aesthetic-medicine lead funnels puts the half-life of intent at under 5 minutes.

  • Target SLA: first call within 5 minutes of form submit, 7am-10pm UK time, 7 days a week.
  • Fallback: if no answer, WhatsApp message within 10 minutes (template: "Hi [Name], it's [X] from Restart Medical re your P-Shot enquiry. Best time to chat?")
  • Day 1 cadence: 3 call attempts + 1 WhatsApp.
  • Day 2-7: daily WhatsApp drip + one call/day until contact or 7-day stop.
  • What to ask Allen / Yas: what's the current SLA? If > 1 hour, this is the single biggest lift available, bigger than any ad change.

12Lookalike & audience experiments

53 won leads is enough to build a usable lookalike, but only just. Keep cold broad audiences as the workhorse and run lookalikes as a small parallel test.

Upload the 53 won-lead emails + phones as a source audience. Build a 1% UK lookalike. Run with Winner Video_11 at £15/day, geo-limited to Barnsley + 25 km. Compare CPL to Campaign A after 7 days.

Test 2 — Interest-stack cold audience

Cold broad with a soft interest filter: Men 40-65, interests include "Men's health" OR "Erectile dysfunction" OR "Testosterone" OR "Andrology" OR "Aesthetic medicine". £15/day. Goal: see if interest signal beats pure broad.

Test 3 — 3% UK Lookalike of LP visitors (engaged, not yet leads)

Once your LP has 1,000+ visitors logged in the pixel, build a 3% lookalike of "LP visited, did not lead" + the won-customer LAL. £15/day. This often beats pure won-customer LAL because the source is larger.

What not to do yet: stacked detailed targeting (more than 2 interest filters). Meta delivery is now best with broad inputs. Adding 5 interest layers chokes scale.

  • Run Angle 2 + Angle 5 as parallel creatives in Campaign A.
  • Open Tier 4 geo expansion (Bradford, Birmingham, Leicester, Nottingham) at £30/day.

  • If LTV math holds (CAC < 25% of booked treatment value), push to £250-300/day.
  • If CPL is climbing past target, freeze spend and refresh creative before scaling further.
  • By end of week 4 you should have 5 working creatives, 2-3 winning ad sets, and a clear scale picture.
  • Target: 30+ wins for week 4 alone, 90+ wins for the month.

17The math you need to sanity-check before scaling

None of the above matters if the unit economics don't work. Before pushing past £100/day, answer:

  • What's the average P-Shot treatment value? (Need this to set max CAC.)
  • What % of won leads = booked + paid? The GHL "Won" stage = paid. But what's the lead → booked rate? Lead → paid rate?
  • What's the LTV? P-Shot often pairs with follow-up sessions, partner referrals, other treatments. A patient is worth more than one transaction.
  • What's the max CAC you'd accept? Rule of thumb for medical aesthetics: CAC < 25-30% of first-treatment value, or you lose money even with strong LTV.

Once those four numbers exist, the scale ceiling becomes mechanical. Until they exist, treat all spend over £100/day as exploratory.
