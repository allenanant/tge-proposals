Angle 4

Why One Device Doesn't Fix Three Problems

Built on: "I was never warned before that there would be indents though, I was expecting just to have red new skin." Contrarian angle that captures the woman who already tried this elsewhere and got disappointed.

Script 4A
Reel · 35s
Hook (0–3s)
If you had plasma pen somewhere else and the result disappointed you, the technology isn't what failed.
Body (spoken, clinical tone)
If you had plasma pen somewhere else and the result wasn't what you wanted, the technology isn't usually the problem. One device on every patient is. Your upper lids, your tear troughs, and the crepey skin under your lashes are three different problems. They need three different treatments in the same appointment. I sequence them by zone, in one sitting. That's the difference. If a previous treatment left you worse off, come for a free consultation. We'll look at all three areas before we touch anything.
CTA
Free consultation. Harley Street.
Shot cues
  1. 0–6s: Dr SW avatar direct to camera, no B-roll, no music. Letting the line land.
  2. 7–15s: Clean anatomical diagram or model. Three eye zones get circled one at a time.
  3. 16–22s: Soft B-roll of clinic equipment for each zone treatment (brief, not gear-porn).
  4. 23–32s: Real before-and-after from drswclinics.com (pick a multi-zone case, not just upper-lid).
  5. 33–35s: CTA card. Free consultation. Harley Street.
Why it works. Captures the segment everyone else has already lost. Women who tried this elsewhere and got nothing for their money. Authority through honesty.

Angle 5

Nobody Should Be Able To Tell

Built on: Coveteur's "still look like themselves" + Allen's existing Tatyana, 55, London testimonial. Directly addresses the only objection left after price and trust: "I don't want anyone to know."

Script 5A
Reel · 28s
Hook (0–3s)
"You look well." "Did you change your hair?" "Have you been sleeping more?"
Body (mix of vox-pop + Dr SW voiceover)
Open with vox-pop: a daughter, a friend, a husband, a colleague — each says one line. "You look well." "Did you change your hair?" "Have you been sleeping more?" "Holiday?" Cut to the patient at the end, smiling. Dr SW voiceover: "Non-surgical blepharoplasty, done properly, doesn't get noticed. It gets misattributed. People think you slept well. They ask if you've cut your hair. That's the brief. If a treatment makes people stare, it was the wrong treatment."
CTA
Book a free consultation. Harley Street.
Shot cues
  1. 0–6s: Four quick cuts of different people speaking direct to camera, each with one line. Handheld, daylight, real-feeling.
  2. 7–12s: Patient on screen, smiling slightly, in her own clothes (not clinical setting).
  3. 13–22s: Quiet B-roll of clinic, gloved hands, no procedure footage.
  4. 23–26s: Cut to a real before-and-after from drswclinics.com — pick a subtle, refined result, NOT a dramatic one.
  5. 27–28s: CTA card.
Why it works. She's terrified of looking "done." This sells plausible deniability as the actual product.
