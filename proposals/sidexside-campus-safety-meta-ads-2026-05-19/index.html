Research summary

The category is loud and the gap is obvious.

The category splits into three lanes that don't talk to each other. Life360 owns parent peace-of-mind through expensive comedic catastrophe creative. Rave Guardian, AppArmor, and LiveSafe sell admin dashboards with feature-checklist B2B copy. Consumer women's safety apps lean on a tired "girl walking alone at night with SOS button" trope. Nothing owns peer-to-peer companionship, nothing speaks to the mundane 9pm library walk, and the female student herself is never treated as the buyer.

What got rewritten and why. Six scripts hit "not X, it's Y" structures, banned platitudes, or AI-rhythm parallelism on first draft and were rewritten using customer-quote language. The biggest cleanups were: 1.2 (removed an inverted parallel ending), 2.2 (broke a tricolon by varying clause length), 2.3 (replaced a closing platitude with a concrete swap), 4.2 (rewrote "students open apps that get them somewhere, not the apps for what might go wrong" into a non-banned construction), 4.3 (replaced "they don't need X, they need Y" with a reframe), 5.3 (removed "the question isn't X, it's Y").

Gap to flag. Neo got blocked on Reddit and Quora (403 on both via WebFetch). The student quote bank is solid but would be sharper with raw r/college and r/TwoXChromosomes language. Worth running a logged-in browser-harness pull on Allen's Mac if these ads need a v2 round.

Validate before content team builds

  • Confirm the "12 minutes between leaving and home" is a credible average walk time for the target campuses. If most are sub-15-min walks, the number lands. If sub-7-min, tweak to "the few minutes between."
  • Confirm SideXSide UI screenshots used in mockups match the current production UI (the agent assumed map view, named student dots, "Walking now" status).
  • The "13% DAU" admin stat is a credible range from competitor reviews but not sourced to a specific app. Either footnote with "industry average for procured campus safety apps" or pull a real datapoint from a competitor case study before going live.
  • SideXSide brand is blue #076cc7 + yellow #fef200 + Inter. Mockups above assume purple SxS map-pin colour. Swap purple highlights for the SxS blue at design time.
