By the time an email arrives most clients are already on the call. WhatsApp is the right channel for this beat.

Setup notes for GHL

  • WhatsApp variable {{1}} = first name. If first name is missing, use a fallback template that drops the Hi {{1}}, line.
  • Email merge fields used: {{first_name}}, {{call_date}}, {{call_time}}, {{call_link}}.
  • One CTA URL button per WhatsApp message. Calendar link goes in once Allen provides it.
  • Footer text on WhatsApp templates: Restart Medical.
  • Test with one internal phone + one internal email before activating live.
  • Flow A and Flow B are mutually exclusive. Booking triggers exit from A and entry into B.
  • External reschedule or cancellation should trigger a separate (future) flow.
  • Exit conditions for both flows: contact replies (human handover), STOP / unsubscribe, marked unsuitable / duplicate / invalid.

Compliance and voice guardrails

  • Flow A submits as MARKETING templates. Flow B submits as UTILITY templates (transactional reminders for a confirmed booking).
  • STOP / unsubscribe path on every WhatsApp and every email.
  • No before / after claims. No "guaranteed" anything. Outcome references qualified with "where suitable" or "what's realistic for you".
  • No pathologising the customer's appearance. Treatment framed as an option, not a fix.
  • Alan's RN status referenced accurately. No "doctor" or "surgeon" framing.
  • Calm, short sentences, no jargon. No "do you suffer from", no "are you struggling".
  • No em-dashes anywhere in body copy or subject lines.
  • Flow A is signed personally from Alan where it fits, which lifts trust and reply rate in clinical contexts.
